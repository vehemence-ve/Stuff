Day One

Hello, Mortals.

Hello there, mortals. Welcome to whatever this “blog” thing is supposed to be. My son, Kevin, insisted I start one because, apparently, my life is “super interesting and totally worth sharing.” Personally, I think my life’s about as exciting as watching lava harden, but if it makes him happy, fine.

Oh, right — introductions. I’m Vehemence, Sin of Wrath, Ruler of the Wrathful Cities, and general keeper of all things loud, violent, and molten. And before you ask — yes, that Wrath. No, I don’t know your cousin’s exorcist.

Kevin, my son, is sixteen and my heir to the throne. You might be thinking, “But wait, you’re immortal! Why retire?” Because, my dear mortals, immortality doesn’t mean I enjoy spending eternity on paperwork and demon HR meetings. I’ve been King long enough to know that ruling Hell is just eternal customer service with more fire.

My Day

Morning

I don’t sleep — perks of being a Sin — so I spent most of the night handling administrative duties. When morning came, I decided to relax for once, but then ding! Meeting time with the siblings.

You’d think after a few eons we’d have figured out how to hold a meeting without yelling. But no — it’s the same every time. Pride talks too much, Envy interrupts, and Sloth’s probably napping in a corner. Shockingly, it went smoothly today. We mostly renewed a few soul contracts and argued over borderlines no one actually cares about. Bureaucracy, even in Hell.

Afternoon

Once I got back to the Wrathful Cities, I checked in with the generals. Surprise, surprise — two demons were fighting over a lava pit the size of a mortal backyard. I let them duel it out. Keeps morale up.

Meanwhile, Kevin said he was “going to the mortal realm to study human culture.” Which is code for “he’s hitting a drive-thru again.” Last time he came back with a milkshake and said it “changed his life.” Kid, you live in Hell. Nothing’s cold here.

Evening

Evening was quiet — relatively speaking. The souls in the pits were screaming louder than usual, so I turned down the temperature. I’m not going soft; I just hate background noise.

Kevin came back bragging about his “followers.” I don’t get it. Mortals actually enjoy being followed? Down here, that usually ends with screaming and fire. But I smiled, nodded, and pretended to understand “content creation.” Parenting win.

Closing Thoughts

So, that was my day. Ruling Hell: 1% glory, 99% maintenance. But I’ll admit — it’s not boring.

Maybe this “blogging” thing will be a decent outlet. If you mortals actually enjoy hearing about my infernal nonsense, congratulations — you’re probably bound here anyway.

Until next time, behave yourselves… or don’t. I’ve got plenty of room down here either way.

— Vehemence, Sin of Wrath
